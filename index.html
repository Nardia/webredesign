Benefits of Honey
Rich in Antitoxins
Many of us enjoy honey and incorporate it into our daily diet, whether as anatural sugar substitute for our coffeee or simply as a sweet treat. But specific honey varieties known as "active" honeys have been als been utilised throughout history for thgier antibacterial and medicinal properties.
Natural Cough Medicine
Honey is time-honored remedy for a sore throat. According to one study it can also relieve coughs more than OTC medications that contain dextromethorphan(DM) , a cough suppressant. You can create your own remedy at home by mixing up 2 teaspoons of honey with herbal or warm water and lemon.

Lower Blood Pressure
Blood pressure is an important risk factor for heart disease abd honey may help lower it. This is because it contains antioxidants compunds that have been linked to lower blood pressure. Studies in both rats and humans have shown modest reductants in blood pressure from consuming honey.
Improves Cholesterol
Studies have shown that consuming honey lowers LDL (bad) cholesterol
by 6-11% and lowers triglyceride levels by s much as 11%. Honey may
also increase HDL (good) cholestero; by about 3%

Improves Heart Health
It's high antioxidants such as phenolic acids and flavonoids, which may support better health. Small studies in humans show honey's antioxodants could help improve cholesterol levels, whcih could help decrease the risk for heart disease although larger long-term studies are needed to confirm those findings.

Antibacterial properties
Honey contains hydrogen which may account for some of its antibacterial properties. It also has a high sugar content, which help stop the growth of certain bacteria. Additionlly honey has a low pH level. This works to pull moisture away from bacteria causing bacteria to get dehydrated and die off.
